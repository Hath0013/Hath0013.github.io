Euna Solutions (formerly GTY)

Design System Build | Landing Pages

OVERVIEW

Euna Solutions consolidated and rebranded in May 2023 which posed numerous design questions and challenges. I was tasked with creating a seamless and efficient Design System for Euna's Unbounce landing pages which could be used easily by the Digital and Design teams.

ROLE

Graphic Designer & Developer

Design System, User Interface, User Experience, Interaction, Visual Design, Front-end Web Design, Prototyping & Testing

Background

Previously 9 different companies, Euna Solutions delivers best-in-class solutions that keep the public sector thriving. Launched in May 2023, different public sector software companies consolidated to offer a platform of products. Our design team was made up of 3 people, on a Marketing team of about 40.

I designed and implemented design systems and structures.

In addition to day-to-day graphics, I focused on designing design systems at Euna. I learned about SaaS and Marketing, during this time I was able to accomplish these key achievements:

  • Designed and implemented a landing page Design System. This was key in elevating Euna's new brand and bridging the Digital and Design teams.
  • Implemented a file and folder system for design. This system established cohesiveness and consistency among our internal design team and could also be used by external designers.
  • Built, distributed and maintained brand libraries. I built Adobe Creative Cloud libraries to be accessible by anyone with an Adobe account. This was critical in creating brand consistency with the Marketing & Comms teams.
  • Mentored and art-directed Junior Designers. Providing support and art direction for Junior Designers was a new experience and strengthened my team-building skills.

Understanding the problem

Rebranding Euna's landing page templates presented the opportunity to improve the current system. Up until this point, Digital was putting pages together for Design to review. That process proved to be inefficent, frustrating and disjointed which also led to brand inconsistency. This was a great a use case to implement a Design System to bridge Digital and Design, allowing the teams to work cohesively, fluidly and efficiently together.

My research encompassed:

  • Identifying what makes a good Design System work
  • Knowing what can lead a Design Systems to failure
  • Understanding what current Design Systems are available
  • Learning more about Unbounce's capability and functionality

Defining system requirements

I worked with Design to Identifying all the potential system requirements and presented these to the Digital team.

Together, we defined these key system requirements:

  • Be easy for Design and Digital to maintain
  • Be accessible, efficient and easy to use for both teams
  • Include a library of pre-formatted graphics for Digital to use
  • Be accompanied by documentation in the form of Design System Rules
  • Ensure that each landing page category follows a colour pathing system
  • Follow a simple structure for easily building future page templates

Process: design mockups and testing

These are the next steps I took to at this stage:

  1. Sketch layouts, develop low-fi mockups in Illustrator
  2. Design Sync: low-fi mockups
  3. Present to Digital: hi-fi mockups
  4. Design: Build and test one mockup in Unbounce
  5. Hand to Digital: Unbounce mockup for testing and review
  6. Integrate feedback from Digital
  7. Iterate on steps 4-6 until all landing pages are complete

Finalizing and implementation

Living SharePoint asset library

A key functional component of the Design System was a living SharePoint asset library. The purpose of the library made it easy for Digital to find and use landing page assets like photos, icons and graphics. Design was responsible for maintaining this library.

System rules and style guides

Another component of the Design System was a Visual Guide outlining responsibilties, global and category-specific rules, colour pathing examples, locations to corresponding assets within the SharePoint asset library.

A snapshot of the Design System Rules.

Results and takeaways

Post-implementation, Design and Digital continued to sync to identify and solve any pain points. We continued to work together to improve the new system and got a lot of great feedback from the marketing team on the new Design System and landing pages.

Some key takeaways from the designing the Landing Page Design System:

  • End users can be internal, not just potential clients. In designing this system, we were prioritizing the needs and pain points of the Design, Digital and Marketing teams as well as users visiting the landing pages. It's important to recognize the same UX principles should be applied internally and externally.
  • Introduce ongoing communication. Before the Landing Page Design System was in place, Design and Digital had almost no ongoing communication other than campaign kickoffs. Introducing a bi-weekly touchpoint strengthened team efforts.
  • Testing doesn't end after development. Design is a constant iteration of improving the experience for the end user. Always find ways to collect and listen to your user's feedback.
