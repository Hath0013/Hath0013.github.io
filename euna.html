OVERVIEW

Previously 9 different companies, Euna Solutions delivers best-in-class solutions that keep the public sector thriving. Launched in May 2023, different public sector software companies consolidated to offer a platform of products. The big idea: forward-thinking solutions that have your back.

ROLE

Graphic Designer & Developer

User Research, Interaction, Visual Design, Front-end Web Design, Prototyping & Testing

Background

I supported a massive rebrand at Euna.

I joined Euna Solutions in 2022 prior to it's rebrand in 2023, originally with Questica (now known as Euna Budget) and worked primarily with the Budgeting & Grants business units. When the company consolidated it's 6 individual business units into one, my design support grew to span 6 different product lines; Procurement, Permitting, K-12, Payments, Budgeting and Grants.

I've learned so much about SaaS and marketing during my time at Euna, I've listed some of my highlights below:

  • Establish design consistency. Bringing visual cohesiveness to the design projects allowed the brand to communicate more clearly.
  • Learn to advocate for yourself. Self-advocacy also helped me create independence and empowered me to find solutions to problems that others might not have been aware of. This meant learning to understand my needs, knowing what kind of support I needed, and communicating those needs to others.
  • Know your tools inside & out. It pays to learn as much as possible about the tech stack you use on a daily basis and it's capabilities. Sharing this knowledge with others helps streamlining their processes as much as your own.
  • Learn from the team.Â Marketing is a tough environment to be in if you don't have a good grasp on the different functions. Consistent communication and learning between functions helps everyone to work more fluidly.

Results and takeaways

Some key takeaways from Euna's rebrand are:

  • Plan your work, work your plan. In order to meet the deadline date for the rebrand launch, committing to a strategy and being vigilant with communication served us well.
  • Embrace change. When you're working with so many projects in such a tight timeline, it's best to go with the flow and embrace the many changes that come with the territory.
  • Immerse yourelf in the process. Being part of the process of building and shaping the visual identity was a huge benefit when it came time to apply the new brand.
