Canadian Science Publishing

Sales & Marketing Strategy | Platform Design

OVERVIEW

After Canadian Science Publishing (CSP) rebranded, they needed a design solution to promote their new identity to current and potential institutions. In addition, CSP needed a reliable system to gather data and measure performance. The goal of this project was to research and implement a design solution that enabled marketing to promote CSP’s brand identity, track campaign performance and use that data to inform future sales and marketing decisions.

ROLE

Graphic Designer

User Research, Interaction, Visual Design, Front-end Web Design, Prototyping & Testing

Background

Canadian Science Publishing is an Ottawa-based not-for-profit scholarly publisher that provides access to their suite of scientific journals through paid and open access subscriptions. Learn more about Canadian Science Publishing here.

I was the whole design team at CSP.

I joined CSP as a graphic designer two years ago in a company of over 40 scientific publishing editors, editorial assistants and managing editors. I support UX and UI across every aspect of CSP's business and am responsible for leading visual design and branding across key parts of the corporate website, Wordpress blog site, social media and communications.

I've grown tremendously in the last two years, some key achievements of which I have listed below:

  • Implementing a design process. This has helped our team establish more structure to how we conduct our work.
  • Initiating usability testing of our sales and marketing tools. Since no usability tests were previously conducted, we started conducting testing and used the feedback to either improve or replace our current tools.
  • Implementing tools with built-in metrics. This has given our team a way to measure our successes and inform future decisions based off data.
  • Improving brand and visual presence across all channels. We had little to no brand presence when I came on board, librarians and agents have become aware of us since we started promotional campaigns.
  • Establishing a design system. This has helped Operations and Sales to understand how and why we choose to implement certain components over others.

Understanding the problem

When I joined the team, there was no solution in place for marketing to measure performance metrics of sales-specific campaigns. As a result, there was no way for marketing to know if these campaigns were successful. This caused a gap between marketing and sales because marketing could not report on current and past campaigns or inform future campaigns using data. It also meant that marketing could not support sales to its fullest extent.

My research encompassed:

  • Understanding the user goals and needs
  • Uncovering pain points with the existing user journeys
  • Methods to determine the success of the tasks measured

Gathering insights

I conducted user interviews with the sales and marketing teams revealed methods for tracking metrics of the sales catalogue, sales campaigns had never been previously implemented. There was no way to collect data and measure the success of sales campaigns or the sales catalogue, other than how many PDF downloads occurred. To get more complex data, new tools needed to be researched and implemented.

The solution had to deliver the following:

  • Provide marketing with a way to track metrics and measure the performance of sales campaigns
  • Empower marketing to fully support sales with the ability to inform future decisions based off data collected
  • Enable the marketing team with a way to promote CSP’s new brand identity and selling points

Preliminary designs

To provide the marketing team with a way to track metrics and measure the performance of sales campaigns, we decided to use Hubspot in combination with Google Analytics. Using Hubspot would also give marketing the ability to promote CSP’s new brand identity. Including testimonials, targeted copy and illustrations of CSP’s new identity would provide an instant snapshot to current and potential subscribers.

By converting the sales catalogue PDF to a digital flipbook, marketing would be able to inform sales of the catalogue performance. Identifying the successful components of the catalogue would help sales make decisions on what content to keep and what to change. To inform the most relevant and necessary content to include on the Hubspot page, I conducted user interviews with members of CSP's primary audience.

Targeted illustrations

To achieve visuals that elevated CSP's new brand identity, I used wordmapping and whiteboarding. This process helped me determine which of the key words in each selling point copy to address in the corresponding visual.

Wireframing the solution

I presented the preliminary designs to the sales and marketing teams for validation. After integrating feedback I began building a prototype in Adobe XD. Functionally speaking the page needed to be interactive and engaging as well as responsive for desktop, tablet and mobile. I built a prototype for each device and included how I would use animation and on-brand visuals to meet these objectives.

I user tested the prototype with marketing, sales as well as CSP’s primary audience. Once the iterative user testing and feedback process was complete, I presented the final prototype to marketing and sales.

Results and takeaways

Since the implementation of the Hubspot landing page and catalogue Flipbook, we’ve seen a significant increase of traffic and engagement in marketing’s sales campaigns. Because marketing has been able to support sales using the data collected, there has also been an increase in subscriptions from librarians and agents sourced from sales-specific campaigns. Furthermore, we have received a lot of positive feedback from our end users on the digital flipbook.

Some key takeaways from this project are:

  • Create a strategic plan. This helps deal with out-of-scope requests that could potentially derail the project and helps deliver a quality product in time.
  • User testing doesn't end after development. Design is a constant iteration of improving the experience for the end user. Always find ways to collect and listen to your user's feedback.
  • Involve sales upfront. This helps to reduce any rework later on as an understanding of the technical limitations upfront will help to inform your design strategy.
